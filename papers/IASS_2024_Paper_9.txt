### **1. System Overview**

The "Unfold Form" system is a reusable, low-tech formwork strategy designed to cast unreinforced, compression-only concrete fan-vaulted floors in situ. The system relies on Curved-Crease Unfolding (CCU), where flat-folded bending-active plates are unfolded into a double-curved, corrugated structure. The corrugations provide structural stiffness to the formwork and create stiffening ribs in the final concrete shell.

### **2. Dimensions and Geometry**

The paper describes a half-scale demonstrator representing a module of a housing unit.

* 
**Overall Plan Dimensions:** 300 cm by 180 cm.


* 
**System Height:** The overall floor buildup height is 40 cm at the four corner supports and 18 cm at the centre crown. Over 70% of the floor depth is below 25 cm.


* 
**Concrete Shell Thickness:** The continuous extrados (top surface) of the shell has a minimum thickness of 4 cm.


* 
**Concrete Rib Depth:** The stiffening ribs (intrados) have a triangular cross-section, varying the structural height of the shell from 4 cm up to 13.5 cm at the deepest centre rib.



### **3. Material Specifications and Fabrication**

#### **The CCU Formwork**

The formwork is split into four symmetric quadrants (two identical, two mirrored).

* 
**Plates:** Each of the four parts is assembled from 14 planar plates made of 8 mm poplar plywood. The plywood's lower stiffness orthotropic direction is aligned with the active bending direction.


* 
**Hinges:** The plates are joined by curved textile hinges made from 14 developable strips of industrial woven polyester textile.


* 
**Textile Coating:** The textile has a one-sided PVC coating facing the concrete to ensure a clean finish and allow reuse without release agents. The uncoated side adheres to the plywood.


* 
**Assembly:** The textile strips are sewn together using industrial thread. The plywood plates are attached to the textile using standard double-sided tape. To handle tangential forces at the hinge, the textile perimeter flaps are wrapped around the edges of the plywood and secured with staples.


* 
**Weight:** Each prefabricated quarter weighs 6 kg, totalling 24 kg for the entire formwork.



#### **The Support Frame**

* 
**Material:** The substructure frame is made of 24 mm plywood plates.


* 
**Design:** It features zigzag-shaped plates along the crown lines and ledges along the perimeter to precisely position and support the CCU quarters. The sidewalls act as edge moulds for the wet concrete.


* 
**Connections:** The frame is assembled via reversible bolted connections and hinges. The CCU quarters connect to the frame using industrial velcro fasteners.



### **4. Computational Co-Design Workflow**

To replicate the form-finding and analysis, the authors utilized the following specific software and methods:

* 
**Geometric Design:** The funicular target geometry of the fan vault is generated using the Thrust Network Optimisation (TNO) package within the COMPAS framework.


* 
**Formwork Generation:** The "reflection method" translates the funicular target into the specific corrugated bending-active plate geometry of the CCU.


* 
**Structural Analysis:** Finite Element Analysis is conducted in SOFISTIK. The curved-crease hinges are modelled with spring connections. The analysis assesses serviceability limit states (SLS), ultimate limit states (ULS), push-over, and buckling to capture snap-through behaviour and internal stresses from active bending.


* 
**Fabrication Design:** The 2D cutting patterns for the plates and textiles are computed by simulating the close-folding mechanism of the CCU.



### **5. Concrete Mix and Casting Procedure**

The system uses a single-sided mould, meaning there is no top counter-mould.

* 
**Mix Design:** The concrete requires a non-fluid consistency so it can be manually shaped on the vaulted extrados without sliding. It utilizes a highly calibrated water-cement ratio and includes renewable kenaf fibres to improve consistency, flexural strength, and mitigate shrinkage cracking.


* 
**Casting Process:** Concrete is cast in two layers. The first layer fills the triangular ribs and is compacted using vibrating needles. The second layer forms the continuous shell.


* 
**Shaping:** The top surface (extrados) is manually shaped and compacted using vibrating masonry trowels, following custom timber guides to maintain the strict 4 cm thickness.


* 
**Curing & Decentring:** Decentring (formwork removal) occurs after 3 days of curing. Horizontal thrust during casting and the lifecycle of the vault is restrained by tension ties connecting the column capitals.



### **6. Final Floor Build-up Stratigraphy**

Above the unreinforced concrete shell, the floor system is levelled and finished with the following layers (from bottom to top):

1. 
**Dry Fill:** For horizontal levelling, load distribution, and insulation.


2. 
**Fibre Board:** For additional insulation.


3. 
**Dry Screed:** For further load distribution.


4. 
**Rubber Floor Finish:** The final topmost layer.
